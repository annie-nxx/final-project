Vovinam: Vietnamese Martial Arts

"Revolution To The Mind"

Nguyen Loc

Vovinam aka Vietnamese Martial Arts, founded by Nguyen Loc in 1936 but was silent until 1938. It was created in order to free Vietnam from the ruling of the French. Synthesized from Kung-Fu, traditional Vietnamese Martial Arts, and the Japanese and Korean systems, Vovinam was created, the purpose of this martial art was to create a national identity for the Vietnamese people from being occupied by the French. Vovinam uses the force and reaction of the opponent, it is based on the hard and soft principle, as in, how forcefully a defender counters the force of an attack that is armed and unarmed, and it also trains your mind and body. Vovinam practices with and without weapons. The weapons that are used when teaching are knives, long and short stick, swords, sabre, folding fans, axe, staff and spears. Hands, elbows, legs, knees, and kicks escape and levering, attack and defense techniques are practiced including forms, counter attacks, combat and traditional wrestling. Self-defense techniques are also practice and it covers defenses against weaponless/weapon attacks, chokes, grabbing of the hair/shirt/arm, being bear hugged front/behind etc.

Vovinam Logo

The shape of the logo, a rectangle with a conjoined circle that symbolizes the hard and soft principle, representing harmonic perfection between hard and soft. The yellow map of Vietnam symbolizes our love for one another and relative equality, compatibility, and correspondence.

Meaning of the Colors:

Blue: Stands for the color of the sea and hope, the hope in being successful at Vovinam.

Yellow: Stands for the color of the asian skin, symbolizes the "skin deep" internalization of martial arts.

Red: Stands for the color blood and internal flame, the internal flame is the student internalizing Vovinam even deeper and further.
White: Stands for infiniteness and the bones; symbol of the depth of the spirit.
